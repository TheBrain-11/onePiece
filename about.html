One Piece

One Piece is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Eiichiro Oda. Since July 1997, it has captivated millions of fans worldwide, following Monkey D. Luffy in his quest to find the legendary treasure and claim the title of King of the Pirates.

Synopsis

The story begins with the execution of Gol D. Roger, the man known as the King of the Pirates. Just before his death, he announces that his treasure, the One Piece, is hidden somewhere at the Grand Line. This sparks the "Great Pirate Era," as countless pirates set sail to find it.

The Setting

The world of One Piece is divided into four massive oceans: North Blue, East Blue, West Blue, and South Blue. These are separated by the Red Line, a massive continent-sized mountain range, and the Grand Line, a treacherous sea where the laws of physics often don't apply.

World Powers

  • The Navy (Marines): The military force of the World Government.
  • The Four Emperors (Yonko): The four most powerful pirate captains in the New World.
  • The Revolutionary Army: A group seeking to overthrow the corrupt World Government.

The Straw Hat Pirates

Luffy's crew is a diverse group of specialists, each with their own dreams:

Roronoa Zoro: The swordsman who aims to be the greatest in the world.

Nami: The navigator dreaming of drawing a map of the entire world.

Usopp: The sniper striving to become a brave warrior of the sea.

Sanji: The chef searching for the legendary "All Blue" sea.

Legacy & Impact

By 2022, One Piece had over 516.6 million copies in circulation, making it the best-selling manga series in history. It holds the Guinness World Record for the most copies published for the same comic book series by a single author.
